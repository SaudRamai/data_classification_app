PII - 13 Columns (Most Identifying Data)
-----------------------------------------
These columns DIRECTLY IDENTIFY INDIVIDUALS and require the strictest controls:

1.  SOCIAL_SECURITY_NUMBER - Unique government identifier
2.  TAX_IDENTIFICATION_NUMBER - Tax identity
3.  NATIONAL_ID_NUMBER - National identity
4.  DRIVERS_LICENSE_NUMBER - Government-issued ID
5.  PASSPORT_NUMBER - International identity
6.  VOTER_ID_NUMBER - Voting identity
7.  MILITARY_ID_NUMBER - Military identity
8.  ALIEN_REGISTRATION_NUMBER - Immigration identity
9.  BIOMETRIC_HASH - Biometric identifier
10. VOICE_PRINT_ID - Voice biometric
11. FINGERPRINT_HASH - Fingerprint biometric
12. HEALTH_CONDITION - Protected health information (PHI)
13. DEVICE_LOCATION_HISTORY - Tracking history (more sensitive than single point)

WHY KEPT? These are permanent, unique identifiers that directly link to 
individuals and cannot be easily changed. Exposure creates high risk of 
identity theft, fraud, or privacy violations.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SOX - 9 Columns (Financial Accounts & Transactions)
----------------------------------------------------
These columns PROVIDE DIRECT FINANCIAL ACCESS and require strict financial controls:

1. BANK_ACCOUNT_NUMBER - Direct access to bank account
2. BANK_IBAN - International bank account identifier
3. CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER - Payment card number
4. CREDIT_CARD_EXPIRY_DATE - Card expiration
5. CREDIT_CARD_CVV - Card security code
6. ANNUAL_INCOME - Financial status
7. CREDIT_SCORE - Creditworthiness
8. TAX_RETURN_ID - Tax filing identifier
9. PAYMENT_HISTORY - Transaction records

WHY KEPT? These enable direct financial transactions, fraud, or unauthorized 
access to financial accounts. Critical for SOX compliance and financial audit trails.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SOC2 - 7 Columns (Authentication Credentials & Secrets)
--------------------------------------------------------
These columns GRANT SYSTEM ACCESS and require strict security controls:

1. USER_PASSWORD_HASH - Authentication credential
2. API_KEY - System access key
3. API_SECRET - API authentication secret
4. OAUTH_TOKEN - OAuth access token
5. OAUTH_REFRESH_TOKEN - OAuth refresh token
6. TRADE_SECRET_KEY - Proprietary business secret
7. ENCRYPTED_MESSAGES - Confidential communications

WHY KEPT? These provide direct access to systems, applications, or confidential 
business information. Compromise leads to unauthorized access, data breaches, 
or intellectual property theft.

================================================================================
CLASSIFICATION CRITERIA
================================================================================

‚úÖ CRITICAL (Kept) - Columns that:
-----------------------------------
- Directly identify individuals (SSN, passport, biometrics)
- Provide direct financial access (bank accounts, credit cards)
- Grant system access (passwords, API keys, tokens)
- Cannot be easily changed (biometrics, government IDs)
- Create immediate risk if exposed (fraud, identity theft, unauthorized access)

‚ùå LOWER RISK (Removed) - Columns that:
----------------------------------------
- Are generic identifiers (customer IDs)
- Are publicly available (routing numbers, SWIFT codes)
- Are context-dependent (IP addresses, device IDs)
- Are duplicative (two-factor phone/email when we have contact info)
- Are reference IDs (agreement IDs, question text)
- Cannot be used alone to cause harm
